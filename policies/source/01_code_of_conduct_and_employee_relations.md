# Code of Conduct and Employee Relations Policy
Category: Employee Relations
Version: 1.0-demo

This is a demonstration policy for the HR Assistant project. An employer must obtain HR and legal review before adopting it.

## Respectful workplace

All employees, contractors, managers, clients, and visitors must be treated with dignity and respect. Bullying, intimidation, humiliation, unwanted sexual conduct, discriminatory remarks, repeated hostile communication, retaliation, and abuse of authority are prohibited in the workplace and in work-related digital channels, travel, events, and remote meetings.

Employees are not expected to confront a person whose conduct makes them feel unsafe. A person may set a boundary when comfortable, but reporting does not depend on first confronting the subject of the complaint.

## Reporting harassment or misconduct

An employee may report a concern to their manager, another manager, HR, the designated ethics contact, or the confidential reporting channel. If the manager is involved, unavailable, or creates a conflict of interest, the employee should use HR, the skip-level manager, or the ethics channel.

A useful report includes what occurred, dates and locations, people involved, witnesses, and any messages or documents. Employees should preserve relevant evidence and avoid altering original records. Lack of complete evidence does not prevent a report.

The company should acknowledge the concern promptly, explain the review process, limit information to people who need it, and take proportionate interim safety measures where required.

## Anti-retaliation

Retaliation against a person who raises a concern in good faith, assists a review, requests an accommodation, or refuses unlawful conduct is prohibited. Suspected retaliation should be reported immediately through HR, a skip-level manager, or the ethics channel.

## Immediate safety

If there is an immediate threat of harm, the employee should move to a safe place and contact local emergency services or workplace security. The HR assistant must not delay urgent help by requiring the employee to complete an internal form first.

## Grievances and conflict resolution

Routine work disagreements may be addressed through a manager-facilitated discussion, HR mediation, or a written grievance. Allegations involving harassment, discrimination, violence, retaliation, fraud, or serious misconduct require formal review rather than informal mediation alone.

## Whistleblowing

Concerns involving fraud, bribery, data misuse, financial manipulation, serious safety violations, or concealment of misconduct may be submitted through the confidential ethics channel. Reports should be handled independently when senior leadership may be involved.
