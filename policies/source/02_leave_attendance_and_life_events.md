# Leave, Attendance, and Life Events Policy
Category: Leave and Attendance
Version: 1.0-demo

This is a demonstration policy. Leave entitlements and statutory requirements must be reviewed for the employee's jurisdiction before adoption.

## General leave requests

Employees should submit planned leave as early as practical and provide dates, leave type, duration, and a short business-appropriate reason. Leave is not final until approved unless emergency or statutory rules state otherwise. Leave balances are deducted only after approval.

When an employee asks a question about past leave, the system should retrieve approved leave records for the requested period. It must not start a new leave application merely because the message contains words such as take or leave.

## Bereavement

An employee experiencing the death of a close family member should receive a compassionate response before administrative guidance. The employee may request Bereavement Leave and may combine it with Casual Leave, Privilege Leave, or unpaid leave when policy and balances permit.

The employee should not be pressured to provide detailed personal information in chat. HR may request reasonable documentation only where policy requires it and should allow the employee time to provide it.

## Marriage, childbirth, adoption, and caregiving

Employees may be eligible for Marriage Leave, Maternity Leave, Paternity Leave, Adoption Leave, Miscarriage Leave, or another applicable leave type. The assistant should explain likely options and check balances, but should not assume the employee wants to submit a request until the employee indicates that intent.

## Medical and emergency absence

Employees should prioritize immediate health and safety. They may notify the manager or HR as soon as reasonably possible. The assistant may help record an absence or prepare leave, but it must not provide medical diagnosis or demand sensitive medical details.

## Attendance corrections

Missing punches, incorrect status, or system outages should be reported with the affected date, approximate time, work location, and any supporting information. The manager or HR reviews the correction. A missing punch must not automatically be treated as misconduct.

## Monthly leave history

Queries such as "Did I take leave this month?" or "What leave did I take in June?" are retrieval requests. Return only the employee's approved leave records overlapping the requested month, with total approved days. Pending or rejected requests may be shown separately when explicitly requested.
