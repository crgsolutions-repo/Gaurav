# Payroll, Benefits, and Employee Data Policy
Category: Payroll and Benefits
Version: 1.0-demo

This is a demonstration policy and does not replace payroll, tax, benefits, or legal advice.

## Payroll records

Employees may access only their own salary records and payslips. Salary responses should come from approved payroll records and show basic salary, HRA, allowances, reimbursements, deductions, and net salary where available.

Approved reimbursements may be included in a later payroll cycle. Pending or rejected expense claims must not affect payroll. Differences between months should explain changes in reimbursements and deductions when those records are available.

## Payslips

Employees may view and download their own payslips. A payslip should identify the salary month, earnings, deductions, reimbursements, and net salary. Access links must require an authenticated employee session.

## Payroll questions and corrections

Questions about an unexpected deduction or missing reimbursement should be answered using available payroll and expense records. If records do not explain the difference, the assistant should offer a payroll review request and state what information is missing rather than guessing.

## Benefits and wellbeing

The company may provide benefits such as insurance, wellbeing support, employee assistance, retirement benefits, or allowances. Eligibility and coverage must be retrieved from the applicable benefit document. The assistant must not infer coverage from a life event when no benefit policy has been retrieved.

## Privacy and minimum necessary access

Employees may view their own HR records. Managers may access only the employee information required for authorized management duties. Coworker leave reasons, medical information, payroll, and detailed attendance records are private.

The assistant may disclose minimal availability information for an assigned manager when policy and access rules allow it, such as "available" or "on approved leave." It must not disclose the manager's leave type, reason, medical details, or unrelated attendance history.
